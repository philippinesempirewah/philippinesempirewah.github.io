History

In the northern expanse of Europe, the Republic of Hydrus thrived as a remote yet resilient kingdom during the Napoleonic era. inheritor of a lineage rooted in the traditions of diplomacy, stewardship, and a commitment to preserving the empire's sovereignty.


Surrounded by the upheavals of the Napoleonic Wars, Marshal B. navigated a strategic course for Hydrus, prioritizing neutrality while fortifying the kingdom's defenses. Aware of potential threats posed by Napoleon's ambitions, Brandon forged diplomatic alliances with neighboring nations, endeavoring to shield his realm from the conflict's turmoil.


The Republic of Hydrus, though far removed from the central theaters of war, meticulously prepared for any eventuality. Brandon modernized the military, assembled a disciplined militia, and fortified strategic points along the borders. Amidst these measures, he focused on fostering prosperity, encouraging education, and promoting equality among his subjects.
